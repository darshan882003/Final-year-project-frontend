FAQs about Blood Groups

O- is the universal blood donor, meaning it can be given to anyone in need of blood transfusions.

No, a person with A+ blood group can only donate to other A or AB blood types.

No, a person with O- blood type can only receive blood from O- blood donors.

AB+ is the universal recipient, meaning they can receive blood from all other blood types (A, B, AB, O).

The ABO system classifies blood into A, B, AB, and O types based on the presence of specific antigens, while the Rh system classifies blood as either Rh-positive or Rh-negative based on the presence of the Rh factor.

Yes, someone with AB- blood can donate to a person with AB+ blood, as AB+ can accept blood from all Rh-negative and Rh-positive blood types.
